EMDR Tools

Eye Movement Desensitization & Reprocessing — self-guided tools & psychoeducation

What is EMDR? EMDR is a trauma-focused therapy that uses bilateral stimulation (eye movements, taps, or tones) to help the brain reprocess distressing memories. Research shows it can be as effective as CBT for PTSD and trauma — often faster. Your brain already knows how to heal; EMDR creates the conditions for it to do so.

The 8 Phases of EMDR

PHASE 01
History Taking
Identify target memories, current triggers, and desired future state
PHASE 02
Preparation
Establish safe place, resourcing, and coping skills before processing
PHASE 03
Assessment
Identify the target image, negative belief, emotion, and body sensation
PHASE 04
Desensitization
BLS while holding the memory — let the brain process and shift
PHASE 05
Installation
Strengthen the positive belief with BLS until it fully resonates
PHASE 06
Body Scan
Check for residual tension or distress held in the body
PHASE 07
Closure
Return to equilibrium — always end in a stable state
PHASE 08
Reevaluation
Review progress at the next session, check what remains

Negative → Positive Cognitions

EMDR works with core beliefs. Common negative cognitions and their positive counterparts:

❌ "I am worthless" → ✅ "I have value exactly as I am"
❌ "I am powerless" → ✅ "I have choices. I am in control now"
❌ "I am unlovable" → ✅ "I am lovable and worthy of love"
❌ "I am damaged" → ✅ "I survived. I am resilient"
❌ "It was my fault" → ✅ "I did the best I could. I am not responsible for others' actions"
❌ "I am not safe" → ✅ "I am safe now. The danger has passed"
❌ "I don't deserve good things" → ✅ "I deserve peace, joy, and love"

DBT & CBT Skills

Evidence-based tools for emotional regulation, distress tolerance, and thinking shifts

🌊 Distress Tolerance DBT
TIPP — Rapid Nervous System Reset ▼

TIPP targets your body's physiology to rapidly lower emotional intensity. Use when emotions feel unbearable.

T — Temperature: Splash cold water on your face or hold ice. Activates the dive reflex, slowing heart rate within seconds.
I — Intense Exercise: 30 seconds of jumping jacks or running in place. Burns off stress hormones immediately.
P — Paced Breathing: Exhale LONGER than inhale. Try 4 in, 6 out. Activates parasympathetic system.
P — Paired Muscle Relaxation: Tense each muscle group for 5 seconds, release. Work from feet to head.
ACCEPTS — Distract with Wisdom ▼

When you can't solve a problem right now, distraction is a healthy skill — not avoidance.

A — Activities: Engage in something absorbing — clean, draw, cook, exercise
C — Contributing: Do something for someone else. Text a friend, pray for someone, leave a kind note
C — Comparisons: Compare to a harder time you survived, or to those facing greater challenges
E — Emotions: Create different emotions — watch something funny, listen to music
P — Push Away: Mentally put the problem on a shelf temporarily — give yourself permission to not deal with it right now
T — Thoughts: Engage your mind elsewhere — count, do a puzzle, read
S — Sensations: Strong physical sensations — hold ice, take a cold/hot shower, listen to loud music
IMPROVE the Moment ▼
I — Imagery: Visualize a peaceful place or imagine coping successfully
M — Meaning: Find meaning in the pain — what can this teach you?
P — Prayer: Connect to something greater — God, the universe, hope itself
R — Relaxation: Progressive muscle relaxation, warm bath, aromatherapy
O — One thing at a time: Focus on only this moment, only this breath
V — Vacation: Give yourself a brief mental/physical break — even 10 minutes
E — Encouragement: Talk to yourself like you'd talk to someone you love
🌡️ Emotion Regulation DBT
PLEASE — Reduce Emotional Vulnerability ▼

Your body's state directly impacts your emotional resilience. PLEASE targets the basics that matter most.

PL — Physical illness: Treat and manage physical health. Your body and emotions are one system.
E — Eating: Eat balanced, don't skip meals. Blood sugar swings = emotional swings.
A — Avoid substances: Alcohol and drugs amplify emotional dysregulation.
S — Sleep: Protect sleep like it's sacred. 7-9 hours changes everything.
E — Exercise: Even 10 minutes of movement shifts your emotional baseline.
Opposite Action ▼

When an emotion is driving you toward a behavior that makes things worse, do the opposite — all the way.

Shame → Hide: Opposite — reach out, share with a safe person, hold your head up
Fear → Avoid: Opposite — approach the feared thing (when safe to do so), repeatedly
Guilt → Justify: Opposite — apologize, make amends, accept responsibility
Anger → Attack: Opposite — gently avoid, take space, do kind acts for the person
Sadness/Depression → Withdraw: Opposite — get active, engage, reach out, do pleasant activities
🧘 Mindfulness Skills DBT
Wise Mind — The Space Between Emotion & Reason ▼

DBT teaches that you have three states of mind: Emotion Mind (ruled by feelings), Reasonable Mind (ruled by logic), and Wise Mind — the integration of both. Wise Mind is your inner wisdom, available when you get quiet.

To access Wise Mind: Take a slow breath. Ask yourself: "What do I know to be true, underneath all the noise?" Trust the quiet answer.
Signs you're in Wise Mind: Feels settled, not frantic. Decision feels right in your gut AND your head. You feel at peace with it, even if it's hard.
🔄 Cognitive Restructuring CBT
Thought Record — Challenge Your Mind ▼

Our thoughts create our emotions. CBT helps you examine and reframe distorted thinking.

10 Cognitive Distortions to Recognize ▼
1. All-or-Nothing: "I failed once so I'm a total failure"
2. Overgeneralization: "This always happens to me"
3. Mental Filter: Focusing only on the negatives
4. Discounting Positives: "That doesn't count"
5. Mind Reading: "I know they think I'm stupid"
6. Fortune Telling: "This will definitely go wrong"
7. Catastrophizing: "This is the worst thing ever"
8. Emotional Reasoning: "I feel stupid, so I must be"
9. Should Statements: "I should be over this by now"
10. Personalization: "It's all my fault"

ADHD & Trauma Often Co-Occur

Research shows that early trauma can create ADHD-like symptoms — and those with ADHD are more likely to experience trauma. You are not "too much." Your brain adapted to survive. EMDR, DBT, and proper ADHD support together are a powerful combination. You deserve all three.
