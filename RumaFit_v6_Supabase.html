The Science Behind Your Plan
Formulas · Methods · Rules · Tips
🧠 What Program Are You Running?
Renaissance Periodization (RP) Method
Tyker's program is based on RP methodology — a scientifically-validated training and nutrition system. The core idea: use precise calorie and macro cycling timed around your workouts to simultaneously burn fat and preserve (or build) muscle. Not just "eat less." Smart cycling.
Periodized Cutting — You don't stay in one deficit forever. Calories cycle between Low Days and High Days to prevent metabolism from fully adapting.
Calorie Cycling — Low Days = fat burning. High Days = muscle fuel and performance. The average creates your deficit.
Carb Cycling — The difference between Low and High days is almost entirely carbohydrates. Fat stays capped. Protein stays high. Carbs go up or down.
Depletion + Refeed — You train depleted on Low Days (burns more fat), then refeed on High Days (glycogen goes straight into muscle). Two cycles per week.
NEAT Protection — 10K steps keeps your Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis from collapsing. Your body tries to move less when eating less — steps fight that.
🔢 Your BMR Formula
BMR = Basal Metabolic Rate = calories burned doing absolutely NOTHING. Just existing.
Mifflin-St Jeor (most accurate for women)
BMR = (10 × kg) + (6.25 × cm) − (5 × age) − 161

Your stats:
10 × 101.6 = 1,016
6.25 × 154.9 = +968
5 × 35 = −175
Female constant = −161
Your BMR ≈ 1,648 kcal
Think of BMR as your body's minimum survival bill. Gets paid no matter what — sleeping, working, breathing.
🔢 Your TDEE Formula
TDEE = Total Daily Energy Expenditure = everything you actually burn in a real day.
TDEE = BMR × Activity Multiplier

Sedentary (desk, barely moving) → × 1.2
Lightly active (1-3x/week) → × 1.375
Moderately active (YOU) → × 1.55–1.60
Very active (6-7x hard) → × 1.725

Your restored TDEE ≈ 1,648 × 1.60 = ~2,640 kcal
Currently suppressed to ~2,000 from 6 months of deficit
🔢 The Deficit Formula
TDEE − What You Eat = Deficit
The GAP is what matters. Not the number.
500 cal/day deficit~1 lb/week
750 cal/day deficit~1.5 lbs/week
1,000 cal/day deficit~2 lbs/week
Below 1,400 cals EVERNEVER ❌
3,500 calorie weekly deficit = 1 lb of fat. The math is reliable — but your body will adapt. That's why cycling exists.
🚶‍♀️ NEAT — Why 10K Steps
NEAT = Non-Exercise Activity Thermogenesis. Every calorie burned that ISN'T a structured workout.
5,000 steps/day~250 cals burned
10,000 steps/day ← YOUR TARGET~450 cals
15,000 steps/day~600 cals
The sneaky truth: When you eat less, your brain subconsciously makes you move less. Fidget less. Sit more. Take elevators. Your body tries to close the calorie gap WITHOUT you noticing. 10K steps fights that. It protects your deficit from your own biology.
Going from 5K to 10K steps daily over 5 weeks = ~7,000 extra cals burned = ~2 extra lbs of fat. From steps alone.
📅 Your Current Tyker Schedule — Explained
⬇️ LOW DAYS (1,600 cal) — Sun, Mon, Wed, Thu, Sat
Low glycogen state → body taps fat stores for fuel. Monday workout on Low Day is intentional — training depleted forces fat oxidation. This is the aggressive fat burning trigger.
⬆️ HIGH DAYS (1,900 cal) — Tue, Fri
After Monday's depleted training, Tuesday's carbs go DIRECTLY into muscle tissue for repair — not fat storage. Called nutrient partitioning. Your muscles are primed and hungry.
Weekly Pattern
Deplete (Mon) → Refeed (Tue) → Recover (Wed) → Deplete (Thu) → Refeed (Fri) → Rest (Sat-Sun)
Weekly avg = ~1,657 cals. Weekly deficit against current TDEE = ~2,800-3,500 cals = ~0.8–1 lb actual fat/week.
🥩 Macro Priority Order
Always build your plate in this order. Never skip a step.
1
Protein — 165g minimum FIRST
Build your meals around protein. Everything else fills in around it. Non-negotiable.
2
Fat — stay below 50g
Fat is calorie-dense (9 cal/g vs 4 for protein/carbs). Keeping it low frees up room for more food volume.
3
Carbs — fill remaining calories
Carbs are the lever. Low Days = ~120g. High Days = ~175g. This is what cycles between your two day types.
💡 Tips & Tricks (ADHD Edition)
🍗 Protein hack: Cook a massive batch of chicken Sunday. Eat it all week. Done. Protein solved.
📱 Log as you go: Don't try to remember at night. Log each meal right after eating. Takes 30 seconds.
☕ Coffee in the morning: Black coffee or decaf before breakfast is fine. No calories. Mild appetite suppression. Helps Low Day mornings.
📞 Steps trick: Every phone call = walking. Put your shoes on and walk. That's 1,000-2,000 steps per call. Business call = steps. Done.
🌮 Eating out hack: Grilled protein + vegetables = always safe. Skip the sauce or ask on the side. Rice is fine on High Days.
😵 Scale went up panic? Ask: did I have a High Day yesterday? Am I near my period? Did I eat more sodium? If yes to any — it's water. Wait 2 days.
🚫 The biggest mistake: Cutting calories even lower when you stall. Almost never the right answer. Check steps and protein first. Always.
💤 Sleep is a cheat code: Bad sleep = higher cortisol = more muscle breakdown + more fat storage + more cravings. 7 hours is a non-negotiable macro.

🌸 Period Week Rules
Scale up 2-5 lbs? Normal — water from hormones. Do NOT drop calories. Do NOT panic.
Energy and cravings wild? Add 100-150 cals on High Days. Focus on protein + complex carbs.
Missing workouts from cramps? Light walking still counts. 5K steps > zero. Be kind but still move.
Water retention miserable? Reduce sodium 2-3 days. Drink MORE water not less.
⚖️ Weigh-In Rules
Always first thing in the morning. After bathroom. Before eating or drinking. Same time daily.
Weigh daily, judge weekly. Daily = data. Weekly average = the real story.
Scale goes up on High Days? Expected. Glycogen = water. Not fat. The plan is working.
Never adjust based on 1-2 days. Wait minimum 2 weeks before changing anything.
